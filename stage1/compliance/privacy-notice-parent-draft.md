## What dododo is

dododo lets you share a short video of your child so a **real specialist can look at
it and give you general observations and feedback**. It helps you understand what
you're noticing while you wait for other support.

**dododo is not a medical service.** It does **not** give a diagnosis and is not a
substitute for seeing a doctor or other professional.

## What we collect, and why

- **The video you choose to share.** So a specialist can review it and write you
  observations and feedback. This is the only reason we ask for it.
- **Your consent** (which boxes you ticked, the date/time, and a version number). So we
  have a record that you agreed.
- **A code for your child** (for example `CH-AB23CD`). We **do not ask for your child's
  real name.** We use this code instead.
- **The specialist's written feedback** for you.
- **A short activity log** (who looked, when) so access is accountable. It does not
  contain your name.

We do **not** run any automatic or "AI" analysis on the video. A person reviews it.

## How long we keep it

- **The video: not stored long-term.** Once a specialist has reviewed it, the video is
  **deleted**. If a video is never reviewed, it is automatically deleted after a set
  time.

## Where your information is

Your information stays in the **United Kingdom**. It is **not sent outside the UK**.
The video is **encrypted** while we hold it, and only the **one specialist assigned to
your video** can view it — it cannot be downloaded.

## Your choices and rights

- **Withdraw consent before review** — the video is deleted and not reviewed.
- **Ask us to delete** your video and related information, and **ask for confirmation**
  that it was deleted.
- **Ask what we hold** about you and your child, and **ask us to correct** it.
- You can **complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)** if you're unhappy
  with how we handle your information.
